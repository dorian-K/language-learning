# The Ultimate Spanish Grammar & Pragmatics Cheatsheet
*(Focus: Peninsular Spanish / Spain B1)*

## 1. Core Methodological Principles
*   **The Thinking Method:** Construct sentences piece-by-piece. Do not memorize blindly.
*   **Active Participation:** Pause, build the response mentally, then speak.
*   **Subject Omission:** Verbs indicate the subject. Omit pronouns (*yo, tú*) unless emphasizing or clarifying. (e.g., *Como pan* = I eat bread; *Yo como pan* = *I* eat bread [not you]).
*   **Consistency:** Short daily sessions beat infrequent cramming.

## 2. Phonetics, Orthography, and Stress

### Pronunciation Logic
| Sound / Letter | Rule (Spain Focus) | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vowels** | Constant, sharp, no gliding: **A** (ah), **E** (eh), **I** (ee), **O** (oh), **U** (oo). | *Casa, Jefe, Cine, Foto, Uva* |
| **H** | Always silent (unless in "ch"). | *Ahora* [a-o-ra], *Hablar* [ab-lar] |
| **J / G (soft)** | G before E/I, and all J's make a harsh throat-clearing /x/ sound. | *Jefe, General, Digital, Rojo* |
| **V / B** | Phonetically identical; a soft fricative [β] between vowels. | *Venir, Saber, Variable* |
| **C / Z (Seseo vs. Distinción)** | **Spain:** Z and C (before e/i) make a "th" /θ/ sound. (LatAm uses /s/). | *Cine, Zapato, Cerveza* |
| **R / RR** | Single 'r' between vowels is a tap. Initial 'r' or 'rr' is a rolled trill. | *Pero* (but) vs *Perro* (dog) |
| **QU** | Sounds like "K" (the 'u' is always silent). | *Queso, Quién, Porque* |
| **Ñ** | A distinct nasal sound ("ny", like "onion"). | *Mañana, Soñar, Señor* |
| **E- Prefix** | Words starting with "S + consonant" add an 'e'. | *Especial, Estado, Escuela* |
| **Double Consonants**| Only **rr, ll, cc** exist. English double consonants reduce to singles. | *Posible, Efectivo, Agresivo* |

### Orthography and Stress Rules
| Rule | Stress Position | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Word ends in **vowel, N, or S** | Penultimate (2nd to last) syllable. | *Ha-**bla**-mos*, ***Ca**-sa* |
| Word ends in **other consonants** | Ultimate (last) syllable. | *Ha-**blar***, *Ver-**dad*** |
| **Rule Breakers** | Marked with a tilde (´) on the stressed vowel. | *Lle-**gó***, *Tra-di-**ción*** |
| **Diacritics** | Accents differentiate words with identical spelling. | *Si* (If) vs. *Sí* (Yes) / *Tu* (Your) vs. *Tú* (You) |
| **Interrogatives** | Question words always take accents. | *¿Qué?, ¿Dónde?, ¿Cuándo?, ¿Cómo?* |

## 3. Vocabulary Conversion (English to Spanish Morphology)

| English Suffix | Spanish Suffix | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-tion** (Noun) | **-ción** (Always feminine) | *Organization → Organización*, *Tradition → Tradición* |
| **-al** | **-al** | *Normal → Normal*, *Legal → Legal* |
| **-ant / -ent** | **-ante / -ente** | *Important → Importante*, *Different → Diferente* |
| **-ance / -ence**| **-ancia / -encia** | *Distance → Distancia*, *Difference → Diferencia* |
| **-able** | **-able** | *Probable → Probable*, *Visible → Visible* |
| **-ly** (Adverb) | **-mente** | *Real → Realmente*, *Normal → Normalmente* |
| **-ive** | **-ivo / -iva** | *Positive → Positivo*, *Aggressive → Agresivo* |
| **-tial** | **-cial** | *Essential → Esencial*, *Spatial → Espacial* |

*   **Noun to Verb:** Remove **-ción** and add **-ar** (e.g., *Confirmación → Confirmar*). Exception: *Demostración → Demostrar* (drops the 'n').
*   **Verb Derivations:** 
    *   "-tain" = *-tener* (*Contain → Contener*, *Maintain → Mantener*).
    *   "-pose" = *-poner* (*Compose → Componer*, *Suppose → Suponer*).
*   **Etymological Shifts:** Latin 'F' → Spanish silent 'H' (*Fact → Hecho*); Germanic 'W' → Spanish 'Gu' (*William → Guillermo*). Arabic words often start with **al-** (*almohada, arroz, azúcar*).

## 4. Nouns, Adjectives, and Articles

### Gender and Exceptions
*   **Masculine:** Usually ends in **-o**.
    *   *Exceptions (Greek roots):* End in **-ma** or **-ta** (*El problema, El sistema, El programa, El planeta*).
    *   *Time:* *El día*.
*   **Feminine:** Usually ends in **-a**, **-ción**, or **-idad**.
    *   *Exceptions:* *La mano, La foto, La moto, La radio*.
*   **The "El" Exception:** Feminine nouns starting with a stressed 'A' use *El* for pronunciation flow, but remain feminine for adjectives. (*El agua fría*).
*   **Pluralization:** Add **-s** to vowels, **-es** to consonants (*Doctor → Doctores*).

### Adjectives and Agreement
Adjectives follow the noun and must match in gender and number.
*   *La casa blanca* (The white house) → *Las casas blancas* (The white houses).
*   **Apocope:** Drop the final **-o** before masculine singular nouns: *Uno → Un coche*, *Alguno → Algún día*, *Ninguno → Ningún problema*, *Bueno → Un buen hombre*.
*   **Demasiado (Too much):** Agrees as an adjective (*Demasiadas personas*), invariant as an adverb (*Casas demasiado caras*).

### Demonstratives (This / That)
*   **This/These (Has a 'T'):** *Este, Esta, Estos, Estas*. (e.g., *Este libro*).
*   **That/Those (Drops the 'T'):** *Ese, Esa, Esos, Esas*. (e.g., *Esa mesa*).
*   **Neuters (Abstract/Unknown concepts):** *Esto* (This thing), *Eso* (That thing). 

### The Neuter "Lo"
*   **Lo + Adjective:** "The [adjective] thing." (*Lo importante es...* = The important thing is...).
*   **Lo de:** "The thing about / The business of." (*Lo de la fiesta* = The business regarding the party).
*   **Lo que:** "What" in statements. (*No sé lo que quieres* = I don't know what you want).

## 5. Pronouns: Syntax and Rules

### Pronoun Types
| Person | Subject | Prepositional | Direct (DOP) | Indirect (IOP) | Reflexive |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **I** | Yo | mí | me | me | me |
| **You** | Tú | ti | te | te | te |
| **He/She/Usted** | Él/Ella/Ud. | él/ella/Ud. | lo / la | le (se) | se |
| **We** | Nosotros/as| nosotros/as | nos | nos | nos |
| **You All (Spain)**| Vosotros/as| vosotros/as | os | os | os |
| **They/Ustedes** | Ellos/Uds. | ellos/Uds. | los / las | les (se) | se |

### Pronoun Placement Logic
1.  **Single Conjugated Verb:** Pronoun goes **before**. (*Lo quiero* = I want it).
2.  **Verb + Infinitive/Gerund:** Goes **before** the conjugated verb OR **attached** to the end of the infinitive/gerund. 
    *   *Lo quiero ver* OR *Quiero verlo*.
    *   *Lo estoy haciendo* OR *Estoy haciéndolo* (needs accent to preserve stress).
3.  **Compound Tenses (Haber):** Must go **before** *haber*. Never attach to a participle. (*Lo he visto*).
4.  **Imperative (Commands):**
    *   Affirmative: Attached to end. (*¡Dímelo!*)
    *   Negative: Before the verb. (*¡No me lo digas!*)

### Double Pronoun Rules
*   **Order:** Always **[Indirect] + [Direct]**. (*Me lo das* = You give it to me).
*   **The "Se" Shift (Double L Rule):** If *le/les* is followed by *lo/la/los/las*, the indirect pronoun becomes **se** to avoid the "L-L" sound.
    *   *Le lo di* → **Se lo di** (I gave it to him/her).

### Specific Pronoun Mechanics
*   **Prepositional Exceptions:** After prepositions (*para, por, de, en*), use prepositional pronouns.
    *   *Exception:* *Con* merges into **conmigo** (with me) and **contigo** (with you).
*   **Body Parts & Clothing:** Spanish uses definite articles + indirect pronouns, not possessives. 
    *   *Me duele la cabeza* (My head hurts / "The head hurts me").
    *   *Me pongo los zapatos* (I put on my shoes).
*   **Personal "A":** Requires an 'a' before a specific human direct object. (*Veo a David* vs. *Veo el coche*).

## 6. Ser vs. Estar (To Be)

**Core Logic:** Choice is based on **Classification** vs. **Status**, not permanence.

### Conjugations (Present)
| Subject | Ser (Characteristics/Identity) | Estar (States/Location) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yo** | soy | estoy |
| **Tú** | eres | estás |
| **Él/Ella/Ud.** | es | está |
| **Nosotros/as** | somos | estamos |
| **Vosotros/as** | sois | estáis |
| **Ellos/Uds.** | son | están |

### Usage Rules
*   **Ser:** Identity, inherent traits, profession, material, origin, time/date, definitions.
    *   *Soy médico.* (I am a doctor.)
    *   *El coche es rojo.* (The car is red.)
*   **Estar:** Geographic location (always), transitory physical/mental states, progressive tenses.
    *   *Madrid está en España.* (Madrid is in Spain.)
    *   *Estoy cansado.* (I am tired.)
    *   *La puerta está abierta.* (The door is open.)

### Meaning Shifts with Adjectives
| Adjective | With *Ser* | With *Estar* |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Aburrido** | Boring (*Es aburrido* = He is boring) | Bored (*Está aburrido* = He is bored) |
| **Listo** | Smart / Clever | Ready |
| **Bueno** | Good person / Good quality | Attractive / Tasty / In good health |
| **Cerrado** | Closed-minded | Physically closed |

*(Safety Net: Use "Ser una persona [adjective]" to force a characteristic meaning: "Soy una persona cansada").*

## 7. Core Verb Tenses (Indicative Mood)

### Present Tense (*Presente*)
Used for current truths, habits, and scheduled future events (*Compro el coche mañana* = I am buying the car tomorrow).

| Pronoun | -AR (Hablar) | -ER (Comer) | -IR (Vivir) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yo** | habl**o** | com**o** | viv**o** |
| **Tú** | habl**as** | com**es** | viv**es** |
| **Él/Ud.** | habl**a** | com**e** | viv**e** |
| **Nosotros** | habl**amos** | com**emos** | viv**imos** |
| **Vosotros** | habl**áis** | com**éis** | viv**ís** |
| **Ellos/Uds.**| habl**an** | com**en** | viv**en** |

*   **Stem-Changers (The Boot):** Stems split in all forms except *Nosotros* and *Vosotros*.
    *   **e → ie:** *Pensar* → *pienso, piensas, piensa, pensamos, pensáis, piensan*. (*Entender, Preferir, Venir*).
    *   **o → ue:** *Poder* → *puedo, puedes...* (*Dormir, Encontrar, Mostrar, Volver*).
*   **Irregular 1st Person ("Go" Verbs):** *Tener* (tengo), *Venir* (vengo), *Salir* (salgo), *Poner* (pongo), *Hacer* (hago), *Decir* (digo).
*   **Irregular (Ir):** *Voy, vas, va, vamos, vais, van*.

### Past Tenses: The Point vs. The Line

#### 1. Preterite (*Indefinido*) = The Point
Completed actions with a clear beginning/end. ("I went," "It happened").

| Pronoun | -AR Endings | -ER/-IR Endings | Preterite Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yo** | -é | -í | Hablé, Comí |
| **Tú** | -aste | -iste | Hablaste, Comiste |
| **Él/Ud.** | -ó | -ió | Habló, Comió |
| **Nosotros** | -amos | -imos | Hablamos, Comimos |
| **Vosotros** | -asteis | -isteis | Hablasteis, Comisteis |
| **Ellos/Uds.**| -aron | -ieron | Hablaron, Comieron |

*   **Irregular Preterite Stems:** Use endings without accents (*-e, -iste, -o, -imos, -isteis, -ieron*).
    *   *Estar* (estuv-): *estuve, estuvo...*
    *   *Tener* (tuv-): *tuve, tuvo...*
    *   *Hacer* (hic-): *hice*, ***hizo***.
    *   *Decir* (dij-): *dije*, ***dijeron*** (drops 'i').
*   **Fully Irregular:** *Ser / Ir* (Fui, fuiste, fue, fuimos, fuisteis, fueron); *Dar* (Di, diste, dio, dimos, disteis, dieron); *Ver* (Vi, viste, vio...).

#### 2. Imperfect (*Imperfecto*) = The Line
Background descriptions, habitual past actions, states of being. ("I used to go," "I was walking").

| Pronoun | -AR (Hablar) | -ER/-IR (Comer) | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yo / Él** | habl**aba** | com**ía** | Hablaba, Comía |
| **Tú** | habl**abas** | com**ías** | Hablabas, Comías |
| **Nosotros** | habl**ábamos** | com**íamos** | Hablábamos, Comíamos |
| **Vosotros** | habl**abais** | com**íais** | Hablabais, Comíais |
| **Ellos/Uds.**| habl**aban** | com**ían** | Hablaban, Comían |

*   **Only 3 Irregulars:** 
    *   *Ser:* era, eras, era, éramos, erais, eran.
    *   *Ir:* iba, ibas, iba, íbamos, ibais, iban.
    *   *Ver:* veía, veías, veía, veíamos, veíais, veían.

#### The "Today Rule" (Strict Spain B1 Pragmatics)
In Spain, past tenses are strictly divided by the timeframe context.
*   **Open Timeframe (Present Perfect):** Action in a timeframe not yet over (*hoy, esta mañana, este mes, últimamente*).
    *   *Hoy **he desayunado** pan.* (Today I ate bread).
*   **Closed Timeframe (Preterite):** Action in a finished timeframe (*ayer, anoche, la semana pasada*).
    *   *Ayer **desayuné** pan.* (Yesterday I ate bread).

### Compound Tenses (Haber + Past Participle)
The participle never changes gender/number in compound tenses.
*   **Participles:** -AR → **-ado** (*Hablado*); -ER/-IR → **-ido** (*Comido*).
*   **Irregular Participles:** *Roto* (broken), *muerto* (dead), *abierto* (open), *dicho* (said), *hecho* (done), *visto* (seen), *puesto* (put).
*   **Present Perfect (I have done):** *He, has, ha, hemos, habéis, han* + participle. (*He hablado*).
*   **Past Perfect (I had done):** *Había, habías, había, habíamos, habíais, habían* + participle. (*Ya había comido*).
*   **Conditional Perfect (I would have done):** *Habría, habrías...* + participle. (*Lo habría hecho*).

### Future and Conditional
Added to the **full infinitive** (not the stem).

| Tense | Endings (All Verbs) | Logic | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Future** | -é, -ás, -á, -emos, -éis, -án | "I will" | *Comeré* (I will eat), *Hablarán* |
| **Conditional** | -ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían | "I would" | *Comería* (I would eat) |

*   **Irregular Stems (for both):** *Poder* (podr-), *Tener* (tendr-), *Venir* (vendr-), *Salir* (saldr-), *Poner* (pondr-), *Decir* (dir-), *Hacer* (har-), *Haber* (habr-), *Saber* (sabr-).
    *   *Tendré* (I will have), *Iría* (I would go).

## 8. The Subjunctive Mood

Used for subjective influence, desires, emotions, unfulfilled actions, or unknown entities, usually triggered by *que*.

### Triggers (WEIRDO & Logic)
*   **Wishes/Influence:** *Quiero que...*, *Necesito que...*, *Espero que...*, *Ojalá...*
*   **Emotions/Judgments:** *Me gusta que...*, *Es bueno que...*, *Es terrible que...*
*   **Necessity:** *Hace falta que...*, *Es necesario que...*
*   **Future Temporal:** *Cuando* + [Subjunctive] for future events. (*Llámame cuando llegues* = Call me when you arrive).
*   **Unknowns:** *Busco a alguien que hable español* (I'm looking for someone who speaks Spanish).

### Formation: Present Subjunctive
Swap the thematic vowel. The "yo" form stem from the indicative present is the base.
*   **-AR Verbs:** Use **-e** (*hable, hables, hable, hablemos, habléis, hablen*).
*   **-ER/-IR Verbs:** Use **-a** (*coma, comas, coma, comamos, comáis, coman*).

*   **"Go" Verb Stem Rule:** *Tener* → *tengo* → **tenga**. (*Salga, Ponga, Diga, Haga, Venga*).
*   **Irregulars:** *Ir* (vaya, vayas...), *Ser* (sea, seas...), *Saber* (sepa, sepas...), *Ver* (vea, veas...).

### Formation: Past Subjunctive (*Imperfecto de Subjuntivo*)
Base = 3rd Person Plural (*ellos*) Preterite. Remove **-ron**, add **-ra**.
*   **Endings:** *-ra, -ras, -ra, -ramos, -rais, -ran*. (Always accent the vowel before *-ramos*: *habláramos*).
*   *Hablar* → hablaron → *hablara*.
*   *Tener* → tuvieron → *tuviera*.
*   *Decir* → dijeron → *dijera*.
*   **Usage:** Past triggers (*Quería que vinieras* = I wanted you to come) or Hypothetical "Si" clauses (*Si tuviera tiempo, iría* = If I had time, I would go).

### Workaround to Avoid Subjunctive
Use **Lo + [Adjective] + es que...** to trigger the indicative.
*   *Es importante que **vuelvan**.* (Subjunctive)
*   *Lo importante es que **vuelven**.* (Indicative)

## 9. Commands (The Imperative Mood)

| Person | Affirmative Command | Negative Command |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tú (Informal Sing.)** | 3rd Person Sing. Indicative (*¡Come!*) | 2nd Person Sing. Subj. (*¡No comas!*) |
| **Usted (Formal Sing.)** | 3rd Person Sing. Subjunctive (*¡Coma!*) | 3rd Person Sing. Subj. (*¡No coma!*) |
| **Vosotros (Spain Plural)**| Infinitive: drop -r, add -d (*¡Comed!*) | 2nd Person Plural Subj. (*¡No comáis!*) |
| **Ustedes (LatAm Plural)**| 3rd Person Plur. Subjunctive (*¡Coman!*)| 3rd Person Plur. Subj. (*¡No coman!*) |

*   **Tú Shortcuts ("Go" verbs):** *Tener* (Ten), *Venir* (Ven), *Salir* (Sal), *Poner* (Pon), *Decir* (Di), *Hacer* (Haz).
*   **Vosotros Reflexive:** Drop the 'd', add 'os'. *¡Sentaos!* (Sit down!), *¡Callaos!* (Shut up!). Exception: *Ir* = *¡Idos!* / *¡Iros!*.

## 10. Advanced Verb Structures & Syntax

### "Se" Dynamics
1.  **Passive / Impersonal:** Action "happens" to the subject. Verbs matches the object.
    *   *Se vende casa.* (House for sale).
    *   *Se habla español.* (Spanish is spoken).
2.  **Accidental "Se":** Unintentional events affecting someone. (Se + IOP + Verb matching object).
    *   *Se me cerró la puerta.* (The door closed on me).
    *   *Se nos quemó la cena.* (The dinner burned on us).

### Reactive Verbs (Gustar Family)
Logic: "It is pleasing/seeming to me." Verb agrees with the *thing*, not the person.
*   *Me gusta el coche.* (The car pleases me / I like the car).
*   *Nos gustan los libros.* (The books please us / We like the books).
*   *Me parece bien.* (It seems good to me).

### Verbs of Becoming (*Verbos de Cambio*)
Spanish replaces "to get/become" with specific verbs:
*   **Ponerse + Adj:** Temporary physical/emotional state. (*Me pongo nervioso* = I get nervous).
*   **Hacerse + Noun/Adj:** Voluntary change (profession, ideology). (*Se hizo médico* = He became a doctor).
*   **Volverse + Adj:** Sudden, profound personality shift. (*Se ha vuelto loco* = He went crazy).
*   **Quedarse + Adj:** Resulting state, often a loss. (*Se quedó ciego* = He went blind).

### Essential Structures
*   **Obligation:** 
    *   *Tener que* + inf (Personal requirement: *Tengo que ir* = I have to go).
    *   *Deber* + inf (Moral obligation / "Must": *Debo estudiar*).
    *   *Hace falta* + inf/subjunctive (Impersonal need: *Hace falta agua*).
*   **Aspectual Gerunds & Infinitives:**
    *   *Seguir* + gerund: To keep on doing. (*Sigo trabajando*).
    *   *Terminar* + gerund: To end up doing. (*Terminé vendiéndolo*).
    *   *Terminar de* + inf: To finish a task. (*Terminé de comer*).
    *   *Volver a* + inf: To do again. (*Volví a llamarlo* = I called him again).
*   **Duration:**
    *   *Llevar* + Time + Gerund: (*Llevo 3 meses viviendo aquí* = I've been living here for 3 months).
    *   *Desde hace* + Time: (*Vivo aquí desde hace 3 meses*).
*   **Reported Speech (Backshifting):**
    *   Present → Imperfect: ("Estoy feliz" → *Dijo que **estaba** feliz*).
    *   Preterite → Past Perfect: ("Fui" → *Dijo que **había ido***).
    *   Command → Past Subj: ("Estudia" → *Me dijo que **estudiara***).
*   **Analytical Passive (Formal):** [Subject] + *Ser* + [Participle matching subject] + *por* + [Agent].
    *   *América fue descubierta por Colón.* (America was discovered by Columbus).

## 11. Prepositions and Connectors

### Por vs. Para
*   **Para:** Destination, Recipient, Purpose/Goal ("in order to"), Deadline.
    *   *El regalo es **para** ti.* (Recipient).
    *   *Estudio **para** aprender.* (Purpose).
*   **Por:** Cause ("because of"), Exchange ("for"), Duration, Movement through.
    *   *Gracias **por** venir.* (Exchange/Reason).
    *   *Trabajo **por** tres horas.* (Duration).
    *   *Camino **por** el parque.* (Movement through).

### Verbs with Prepositions
*   *Enamorarse **de*** (To fall in love with).
*   *Pensar **en*** (To think about).
*   *Soñar **con*** (To dream about).
*   *Depender **de*** (To depend on).
*   *Contar **con*** (To count on).

### Relative Pronouns
*   **Que:** That / Which / Who (Default connector, mandatory in Spanish: *Digo que...*).
*   **Cuyo/a/os/as:** Whose. Agrees with the possession, not owner. (*El chico **cuya** casa...* = The boy whose house...).
*   **Prepositional Relatives:** *En la que* (in which), *Con quien* (with whom).
    *   *La chica **con quien** hablé.* (The girl I spoke with).

## 12. Spain & Erasmus Pragmatics / B1 Lexicon

### Expressing Politeness and Ordering
In Spain, directness is polite. Do not overuse conditionals (*Me gustaría...*). Use imperatives or present tense with a friendly tone and "por favor".
*   *¡Ponme una caña!* (Get me a draft beer. Lit: "Put me").
*   *Para mí, un café.* (For me, a coffee).
*   *¿Me cobras? / ¿Qué te debo?* (Can I have the bill? / What do I owe you?).
*   *¿Para ir a la universidad?* (How do I get to the university?).

### Peninsular Discourse Markers (Connectors)
*   **Pues...:** Well... (Thought filler).
*   **Entonces / Por lo tanto:** Then / Therefore.
*   **Sin embargo:** However.
*   **En fin:** Anyway / In short (To wrap up or change the subject).
*   **Vale:** Okay / Understood / Sounds good. (Essential Spanish anchor word).

### Erasmus Survival Vocabulary
*   **University:** 
    *   *La asignatura* (Course module)
    *   *Matricularse* (To enroll)
    *   *Aprobar* (To pass an exam) / *Suspender* (To fail)
    *   *Los apuntes* (Class notes)
    *   *El horario* (Schedule)
    *   *El TFG* (Bachelor's Thesis)
*   **Housing/Logistics:** 
    *   *Piso compartido* (Shared apartment)
    *   *Casero/a* (Landlord)
    *   *La fianza* (Security deposit)
    *   *Gastos incluidos* (Bills included)
    *   *Empadronamiento* (City hall registration)
*   **Slang:** 
    *   *Tío / Tía* (Dude / Mate / Girl - universal informal address)
    *   *Guay* (Cool. *¡Qué guay!*)
    *   *Molar* (To like a lot / be cool. Works like *Gustar*. *Me mola Madrid* = I love Madrid)
    *   *Estar de coña* (To be joking. *¿Estás de coña?*)
    *   *Liarla* (To mess up OR party hard. *Ayer la liamos*)
    *   *Resaca* (Hangover)
    *   *No pasa nada* (Don't worry about it / It's fine - the core cultural philosophy)
